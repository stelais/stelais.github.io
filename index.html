Who am I?

Astrophysicist, Brazilian,

shower singer and chord charmer

Originally from Belo Horizonte, Minas Gerais, Brazil, Dr. Stela Ishitani Silva is a Postdoctoral Fellow at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center. In her research, she explores methods for detecting exoplanets — planets outside our Solar System — and the integration of artificial intelligence for detecting stellar variabilities.

Dr. Stela graduated with a Bachelor's degree in Physics from the Federal University of Minas Gerais and continued her studies, earning Master's and Ph.D. degrees in Physics from the Catholic University of America. During her doctoral period, she was honored with the John Mather Nobel Scholar Award, recognizing her outstanding scientific promise at NASA GSFC, and recently, she received the NASA Postdoctoral Program Fellowship, a prestigious NASA postdoc.

Dr. Stela led the analysis that led to the discovery of the planet MOA-2020-BLG-135Lb. She is a co-author of the discovery of more than 20 exoplanets using the gravitational microlensing technique and more than 180 exoplanet candidates using the transit photometry technique. Among her co-authorships is also the discovery of the first isolated black hole.

Dr. Stela is member of international teams and collaboration, highlighting:

  • The Microlensing Observations in Astrophysics (MOA) collaboration, and
  • The Roman Galactic Exoplanet Survey Project Infrastructure Team (RGES PIT). Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope is a NASA space observatory designed for large-area surveys in the near-infrared, focusing on studying dark energy, dark matter, and microlensing exoplanets. (to-be-launched)

In her free time, she enjoys playing the guitar, singing, and playing kickball.
